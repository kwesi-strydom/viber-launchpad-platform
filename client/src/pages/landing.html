What Viber is

One brand. Four surfaces that feed each other.

Software creation has become entertainment. Viber turns it into a live sport, a storefront, a market, and a movement, pointed at problems worth solving.

Live

Vibecoding competitions, broadcast like sport.

High-intensity, action-packed contests that mirror the entrepreneur's journey.

01
Platform

The Launchpad, where shipped apps get discovered.

A gamified storefront for AI-powered apps and games built on stage.

02
Market

A prediction layer for every competition.

Every spectator gets a betting wallet. The drama becomes a market.

03
Community

Year-round builders, workshops, and creators.

A media network where the next generation of builders ship in public.

04

How an event runs

Not a one-off hackathon. A closed loop.

Real-world problems in, adopted solutions out. Every edition starts by sourcing real business challenges and ends with concrete outcomes: hires, deals, and funding.

Phase 01

Source problems

Real businesses submit live challenges. Builders pick a problem worth solving, not a throwaway demo.

Phase 02

Onboard & cast

Sign up, record an intro, join the group. Audiences meet the cast before doors open.

Phase 03

Build live

Hours of head-down building on partner stacks. Big screens, live commentary, monitored chaos.

Phase 04

Ship & verdict

Products ship to the Launchpad. Audience votes, judge scorecards, prizes across many winners.

Phase 05

Follow-through

Businesses hire the builders, buy the product, or fund what's next. A concrete output.

The Launchpad

Where shipped apps live on.

Every competitor lands here. Audiences, judges, sponsors, and future players follow them in. A flywheel for the builder economy.

01

Gamified storefront

Builders publish AI-powered apps and games. Players explore, rate, play, and share.

02

Reputation leaderboard

Every entry ranked by usage, ratings, and engagement. One scoreboard for the championship.

03

Creator economy

Content creators earn Viber points by promoting apps: memes, teasers, posts, and videos.

04

Self-sustaining cycle

Better apps pull better content, content pulls players, players pull better builders.
